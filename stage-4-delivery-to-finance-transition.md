# Stage 4: Delivery to Finance Transition

## Purpose

Ensure that the financial outcome of the engagement is fully and accurately reflected in the organisation's accounts. It is a handover, not an afterthought. It covers revenue recognition, invoice finalisation, and any retention or warranty period obligations.

---

## Gate 3 Criteria

Before an engagement passes to Finance, the following must be confirmed:

- The Closure Acknowledgement has been received from the client (signed or email-confirmed)
- All timesheets for the engagement have been submitted and approved
- The final invoice has been raised or scheduled
- Any retention amounts and their release conditions are documented
- Revenue recognition entries have been reviewed and confirmed with Finance

---

## Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Delivery Owner | Confirms all delivery obligations are complete, hands over to Finance |
| Finance Partner | Confirms revenue recognition, raises final invoices, manages retention schedule |
| Account Executive | Informed of closure for CRM update and relationship planning |

---

## Key Activities

**Timesheet reconciliation.** All time logged against the engagement is reviewed against the delivery plan. Variances are explained and used to calibrate future estimates, not to attribute blame.

**Revenue recognition review.** The Finance Partner confirms that revenue has been, or will be, recognised in the correct periods and amounts. Any adjustments required are documented.

**Retention management.** Where a retention amount is held by the client, the conditions for release are recorded, the release date is diarised, and the Delivery Owner or Account Executive is responsible for following up.

**CRM and knowledge base update.** The Account Executive updates the CRM with the engagement outcome, key relationships, and any identified opportunities. The Delivery Owner confirms the internal knowledge base entry is complete.
