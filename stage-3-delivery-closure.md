# Stage 3: Delivery Closure

## Purpose

Ensure the engagement ends cleanly from every angle: client, commercial, and people. Poor closure leads to revenue leakage, disputed invoices, unresolved client expectations, and lost knowledge.

---

## Gate 2 Criteria

Before an engagement moves to closure, the following must be confirmed:

- All contracted deliverables are completed or formally accepted
- Outstanding scope items, exceptions, or carve-outs are documented and agreed in writing with the client
- A closure meeting date is set with the client sponsor
- The Finance Partner has confirmed the final billing position

---

## Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Delivery Owner | Leads the closure process, owns the client relationship through to sign-off |
| Project Manager | Confirms deliverable completion and documents outstanding items |
| Finance Partner | Confirms final billing, revenue recognition, and any retentions |
| Account Executive | Joins the closure conversation to support relationship continuity and identify future opportunities |

---

## Key Activities

**Client closure meeting.** A structured meeting with the client sponsor to confirm what was delivered, what was not and why, agreed next steps for outstanding items, and the client's honest view of how the engagement went. Output: Closure Acknowledgement (signed or email-confirmed).

**Internal retrospective (45-60 minutes).** The primary knowledge capture mechanism for the engagement. Output: Delivery Knowledge Base entry.

Retrospective agenda:
- What went well that we should repeat?
- What did not go well that we should change?
- What did we learn about the client, the technology, or the delivery approach?
- Were there any commercial or scope issues to handle differently next time?
- Are there follow-on opportunities to pursue?

**Practitioner transition planning.** Every practitioner's next allocation must be confirmed at least 10 business days before closure. No practitioner should reach their last day on an engagement without knowing where they are going next.

## Template: Closure Acknowledgement (client-facing)

```
Engagement:
Client:
Delivery Owner:
Closure date:

Deliverables confirmed as complete:
[list]

Items not completed and agreed disposition:
[list with agreed next steps, if any]

Outstanding commercial items (retentions, final invoices):
[list]

The client confirms that the above reflects their understanding of the engagement outcome.

Client representative:
Date:
```

| Metric | Target | Frequency | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| % of engagements with completed client closure meeting | 100% | Per engagement | Delivery Owner |

| Disputed final invoices | 0 | Per engagement | Finance Partner |
| Retrospective completion rate | 100% | Per engagement | Practice Lead |
| Days from final delivery to final invoice issued | <5 business days | Per engagement | Finance Partner |
