# Delivery Management Framework

## Purpose

This framework governs how a 50 to 200-person technology professional services and managed services organisation manages the flow of work from the point of sale through to financial close. It is built on the belief that alignment and communication, not process compliance, are what determine whether delivery succeeds.

The framework sits above project methodology. It does not prescribe whether a project runs as Agile, PRINCE2, waterfall, or a hybrid. It creates the organisational conditions for any methodology to work.

---

## Guiding Principles

- **Alignment and communication first.** The root cause of most delivery failures is a gap between what was sold, what was understood, and what was delivered. This framework creates structured checkpoints to close that gap continuously.
- **Leading metrics over lagging metrics.** This framework prioritises metrics that allow conversations before problems become crises: engagement health scores, forecast confidence ratings, scope change velocity, and workforce utilisation trajectories.
- **Probabilistic thinking.** No delivery plan is a commitment. It is a probability distribution. Teams and clients are expected to engage with uncertainty, not be shielded from it.
- **People-first decisions through conversation.** Workforce decisions are not made by formula. This framework creates the data and dialogue to support good decisions. It does not predetermine outcomes.

---

## What This Framework Does Not Do

- It does not prescribe a project management methodology.
- It does not set financial targets independently of the business plan.
- It does not replace judgement. Metrics, templates, and cadences are tools for better conversations, not substitutes for experienced Delivery Owners making informed decisions.

---

## Framework Stages

```
Sales to Delivery Transition
        |
      Gate 1
        |
Project / Engagement / Consulting Delivery
        |
      Gate 2
        |
Delivery Closure
        |
      Gate 3
        |
Delivery to Finance Transition
```

## Stage 1: Sales to Delivery Transition

### Purpose

Ensure that everything learned during the sales process is captured and handed to the delivery team before work begins. Customer confidence is at its highest at the point of signature. This stage is about not wasting it.

### Gate 1 Criteria

- A Delivery Owner has been assigned and briefed
- The client sponsor and key stakeholders have been identified and mapped
- The SOW or engagement brief has been reviewed by the Delivery Owner
- Revenue recognition method and schedule have been agreed with Finance
- A kickoff date and pre-kickoff client meeting have been scheduled
- Assumptions and exclusions from the sale have been documented and flagged

### Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Account Executive / Sales Lead | Delivers the handover briefing; remains available for context |
| Delivery Owner | Accepts the engagement, reviews the SOW, raises concerns before kickoff |
| Practice Lead | Confirms resource availability and team composition |
| Finance Partner | Confirms billing setup, revenue schedule, and PO reference |

### Key Activities

- **Transition meeting (required).** A structured 60-90 minute session between sales and delivery covering: what the client was told, what they care about most, who the difficult stakeholders are, what was left out of scope and why, and what success looks like in the client's eyes. Output: Delivery Handover Record.
- **Risk identification.** Any concern raised by the Delivery Owner or Practice Lead is logged. These are inputs to the first client conversation, not blockers to starting.
- **Team composition brief.** A one-page summary of who will be on the engagement, their roles, and expected time allocation. Shared with the client at or before kickoff.

### Stage 1 Metrics

#### Timing Variables

| Metric | Segment | Type | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| Deal stage for forecast | By size / complexity | Leading | Sales / Delivery Director |
| Mean lead time: close to start | By deal complexity | Leading | Delivery Director |
| Mean lead time: advertise to hire | By seniority band | Leading | Resource Manager |
| Onboarding lead time | By seniority band | Leading | Resource Manager |

#### Capability Variables

| Metric | Description | Type | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| T-shape density as % of population | Proportion of practitioners with a primary deep skill and at least one adjacent skill area (self or manager identified). Higher density = more elastic workforce. | Leading | Practice Lead |

#### Sales and Delivery Alignment Variables

| Metric | Description | Type | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| Core skill population as % of total | % of skill base aligned to primary in-market offer. Higher = stronger alignment of resourcing to sales and marketing effort. | Leading | Practice Lead |
| Deal types with high delivery alignment as % of pipeline | % of sales pipeline aligned to core skill base. Higher = stronger alignment of sales effort to available resourcing. | Leading | Sales / Practice Lead |

> These two metrics together create a two-way reflection. Misalignment in either direction is a leading indicator of revenue leakage (deals closed but can't resource) or profit leakage (resources that can't be aligned to deals).

#### Capacity Variables

| Metric | Description | Type | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| Available hours | Total unallocated productive hours across the delivery workforce in the planning window | Point-in-time | Resource Manager |
| Scheduled hours | Total hours committed to active and confirmed engagements | Point-in-time | Resource Manager |
| Remaining capacity | Available hours minus buffer held for unplanned demand or overruns | Point-in-time | Resource Manager |

#### Deal to Delivery Vehicle Profiling Variables

**Complexity Variables** — rated Low / Medium / High at Gate 1

| Variable | What it measures |
|---|---|
| Complexity | Technical and organisational complexity of the work |
| Team size | Number of practitioners required |
| Criticality | How operationally or strategically critical the outcome is to the client |
| Team location | Co-located, distributed domestic, or distributed across time zones |
| Team maturity | Experience level and prior working relationship within the delivery team |
| Leadership strength | Capability and availability of the engagement's delivery lead |
| Domain knowledge | Degree to which the team holds relevant industry or environment knowledge |
| Dependencies | Number and nature of dependencies on third parties or client teams |

**Uncertainty Variables** — rated Low / Medium / High at Gate 1

| Variable | What it measures |
|---|---|
| Market uncertainty | Degree to which external conditions could affect the engagement |
| Technical uncertainty | Degree to which the technical approach is proven versus experimental |
| Number of stakeholders | Client-side stakeholders with a meaningful interest in the outcome |
| Duration | Planned length of the engagement |
| Level of change control | Formality of the agreed change control process |

**Delivery vehicle indicators:**

| Profile shape | Likely delivery vehicle |
|---|---|
| High complexity / Low uncertainty | Structured project with detailed upfront plan and formal change control |
| Low complexity / High uncertainty | Advisory or consulting model with defined review points and flexible scope |
| High complexity / High uncertainty | Phased program with go/no-go gates; senior Delivery Owner required |
| Low complexity / Low uncertainty | Lightweight engagement model; standard managed service or short-form project |

## Stage 2: Project, Engagement, and Consulting Delivery

### Purpose

Maintain visibility, support the workforce, and keep client relationships healthy across the full delivery lifecycle. The framework's role is not to run the delivery but to create the conditions for it to succeed.

### Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Delivery Owner | Overall accountability for engagement health, client relationship, and team |
| Project Manager / Lead Consultant | Day-to-day delivery management within the chosen methodology |
| Practice Lead | Workforce planning, skills escalation, and delivery quality support |
| Finance Partner | Tracks actuals against forecast, flags revenue risk |
| Resource Manager | Manages allocation across engagements, surfaces utilisation risks |

### Cadence

| Review | Frequency | Participants | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Delivery health check | Fortnightly | Delivery Owner, Practice Lead | Review leading metrics, surface risks early |
| Client business review | Monthly (or as agreed) | Delivery Owner, client sponsor | Relationship health, forward planning |
| Workforce review | Monthly | Practice Lead, Resource Manager | Utilisation, skilling, allocation gaps |
| Portfolio review | Monthly | All Delivery Owners, leadership | Cross-portfolio risk, capacity, and forecast |

### Engagement Health Score

Each active engagement carries a health score reviewed fortnightly. It is a conversation prompt, not a performance grade.

| Dimension | What it measures | Leading indicator |
|---|---|---|
| Scope confidence | How well-defined is remaining scope | Scope change frequency, open change requests |
| Forecast confidence | Reliability of remaining timeline and cost estimate | Estimate revision frequency, dependency blockers |
| Relationship health | Quality of client sponsor engagement | Meeting attendance, response time, sentiment |
| Workforce fit | Whether the right people are on the engagement | Skill gap flags, utilisation variance |
| Commercial health | Whether revenue is tracking to plan | Actuals vs forecast, billing milestone status |

Each dimension is scored Green / Amber / Red by the Delivery Owner with a brief written rationale.

- Amber overall: any single dimension is Amber
- Red overall: any dimension is Red, or two or more are Amber

### Template: Engagement Health Register Entry

```
Engagement:
Delivery Owner:
Review date:
Overall status: Green / Amber / Red

Dimension scores:
- Scope confidence:        [G/A/R] — [brief rationale]
- Forecast confidence:     [G/A/R] — [brief rationale]
- Relationship health:     [G/A/R] — [brief rationale]
- Workforce fit:           [G/A/R] — [brief rationale]
- Commercial health:       [G/A/R] — [brief rationale]

Key risks this period:
Actions agreed:
Next review date:
```

### Workforce and Utilisation

- **Billable utilisation target:** 75-80% for delivery practitioners (excluding practice leads and management). A planning input, not an individual performance metric.
- **Elasticity signals** reviewed monthly: unresolved resource gaps, bench time over 10 business days, skills required but not held, certifications appearing in bids but not in the team.
- **People-first allocation:** practitioners are not reallocated without a direct conversation with their Delivery Owner and the individual concerned.

### Stage 2 Metrics

| Metric | Target | Frequency | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| % of active engagements reviewed fortnightly | 100% | Fortnightly | Delivery Director |
| % of engagements at Green health | — | Fortnightly | Delivery Director |
| Average time to resolve Amber or Red flags | — | Fortnightly | Delivery Owner |
| Scope change velocity (changes per fortnight) | — | Fortnightly | Delivery Owner |
| Billable utilisation (rolling 4-week) | 75-80% | Weekly | Resource Manager |
| Bench time by practitioner | <10 days | Weekly | Resource Manager |
| Active engagements per Delivery Owner | — | Monthly | Delivery Director |
| Client business review completion rate | 100% | Monthly | Delivery Director |

## Stage 3: Delivery Closure

### Purpose

Ensure the engagement ends cleanly from every angle: client, commercial, and people. Poor closure leads to revenue leakage, disputed invoices, unresolved client expectations, and lost knowledge.

### Gate 2 Criteria

- All contracted deliverables are completed or formally accepted
- Outstanding scope items, exceptions, or carve-outs are documented and agreed in writing with the client
- A closure meeting date is set with the client sponsor
- The Finance Partner has confirmed the final billing position

### Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Delivery Owner | Leads the closure process, owns the client relationship through to sign-off |
| Project Manager | Confirms deliverable completion and documents outstanding items |
| Finance Partner | Confirms final billing, revenue recognition, and any retentions |
| Account Executive | Joins the closure conversation to support relationship continuity and identify future opportunities |

### Key Activities

- **Client closure meeting.** Confirms what was delivered, what was not and why, agreed next steps for outstanding items, and the client's honest view of how the engagement went. Output: Closure Acknowledgement (signed or email-confirmed).
- **Internal retrospective (45-60 minutes).** Covers what to repeat, what to change, what was learned, any commercial issues, and follow-on opportunities. Output: Delivery Knowledge Base entry.
- **Practitioner transition planning.** Every practitioner's next allocation confirmed at least 10 business days before closure.

### Template: Closure Acknowledgement

```
Engagement:
Client:
Delivery Owner:
Closure date:

Deliverables confirmed as complete:
[list]

Items not completed and agreed disposition:
[list with agreed next steps, if any]

Outstanding commercial items (retentions, final invoices):
[list]

The client confirms that the above reflects their understanding of the engagement outcome.

Client representative:
Date:
```

### Stage 3 Metrics

| Metric | Target | Frequency | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| % of engagements with completed client closure meeting | 100% | Per engagement | Delivery Owner |
| % of practitioners with confirmed next allocation 10+ days before closure | 100% | Per engagement | Resource Manager |
| Disputed final invoices | 0 | Per engagement | Finance Partner |
| Retrospective completion rate | 100% | Per engagement | Practice Lead |
| Days from final delivery to final invoice issued | <5 business days | Per engagement | Finance Partner |

## Stage 4: Delivery to Finance Transition

### Purpose

Ensure that the financial outcome of the engagement is fully and accurately reflected in the organisation's accounts. It is a handover, not an afterthought.

### Gate 3 Criteria

- The Closure Acknowledgement has been received from the client
- All timesheets have been submitted and approved
- The final invoice has been raised or scheduled
- Any retention amounts and their release conditions are documented
- Revenue recognition entries have been reviewed and confirmed with Finance

### Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Delivery Owner | Confirms all delivery obligations are complete, hands over to Finance |
| Finance Partner | Confirms revenue recognition, raises final invoices, manages retention schedule |
| Account Executive | Informed of closure for CRM update and relationship planning |

### Key Activities

- **Timesheet reconciliation.** All time logged is reviewed against the delivery plan. Variances are explained and used to calibrate future estimates.
- **Revenue recognition review.** Finance confirms revenue has been or will be recognised in the correct periods and amounts.
- **Retention management.** Release conditions recorded, release date diarised, Delivery Owner or Account Executive responsible for follow-up.
- **CRM and knowledge base update.** Account Executive updates CRM with outcome, key relationships, and identified opportunities.

```

```

### Stage 4 Metrics

| Metric | Target | Frequency | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| Days from Gate 3 to final invoice paid | Within agreed payment terms | Per engagement | Finance Partner |
| Retention release rate | 100% within agreed timeframe | Per engagement | Finance Partner |
| Timesheet submission compliance at close | 100% | Per engagement | Resource Manager |
